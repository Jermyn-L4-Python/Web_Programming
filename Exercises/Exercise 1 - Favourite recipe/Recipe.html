Soy Garlic Korean Fried Chicken

As a person who has an unending craving of fried chicken, I have tasted almost every chicken all over the world and nothing beats the crunchy, juicy, spicy, and addictive flavor of korean fried chicken. This is my attempt on recreating one of the most popular and enjoyable food of korean cusine that will leave you wanting for more.

Ingredients

  • 2 - 2.5 lbs of Chicken wings/Drums
  • 3 tbsp. Rice Wine
  • 1 tsp. grated Ginger
  • 2 tsp. grated Garlic
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup All Purpose Flour
  • 1/4 cup Potato or Cornstarch
  • Salt & Pepper, to taste
  • Neutral oil, for frying

Soy Garlic Sauce

  • 2 tbsp. Butter
  • 1/4 cup Soy Sauce
  • 4-5 minced Garlic cloves
  • 2 tbsp. Honey
  • 1 tbsp. Brown Sugar
  • 1 tsp. Red Pepper Flakes
  • 2 tsp. Sesame Oil
  • 1-2 sliced Red/green Peppers
  • Toasted sesame seeds, for garnish (Optional)

Step 1: Season and coat the chicken

  1. Marinate chicken with rice wine, grated ginger, grated garlic, salt & pepper. Set aside for 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  2. In a seperate bowl, combine egg, flour, and cornstarch. Toss in the marinated chicken into the mixture and make sure they are fully coated.

Step 2: Double deep-fry the chicken

  1. Heat neutral oil to 350 degrees F and fry for 7-8 minutes or until the chicken turns light golden brown. Drain the oil on a wire rack or paper towel lined tray and leave to rest for 10 minutes.
  2. Heat the oil up again up to 375 degrees F and fry for 2-3 minutes more or until fully cooked through. Leave it to rest.

Step 3: Make the Soy Garlic sauce

  1. Melt butter in a pan and add in the rest of the ingredients, sitrring frequently until it bubbles.

Step 4: Serving

  1. Toss the double fried chicken into the soy garlic pan, garnish with toasted sesame seeds(optional), and serve to your friends and family to enjoy.
